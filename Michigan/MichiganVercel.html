Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about telehealth in Michigan

Do you accept insurance or HSA/FSA?

We are currently cash-pay only, but we're actively working to enroll with insurance companies and employer HSA/FSA programs. If you'd like to see MichiganDoc covered by your insurance or employer, please ask them to partner with us - it helps our growth tremendously!

What's the difference between synchronous and asynchronous care?

Synchronous care involves scheduled real-time video or phone visits with a provider of your choice. Asynchronous care is faster - the next available provider reviews your case and manages treatment via secure messaging, only contacting you if needed for clinical decisions.

How quickly can I get my prescription?

Most prescriptions are reviewed and sent within 24 hours. You can pick up at your local Michigan pharmacy same-day or use mail order delivery (2-5 business days).

Is my information private and HIPAA compliant?

Yes. All consultations and medical records are fully HIPAA compliant with 256-bit encryption. Your privacy is our priority and we never share your information without consent.

Do you treat patients throughout Michigan?

Yes! We serve all Michigan statewide. Licensed Michigan physicians provide care statewide.

Achieving and maintaining a healthy weight involves understanding the science behind weight loss and implementing proven strategies tailored to your lifestyle.

Understanding GLP-1 Medications

GLP-1 receptor agonists like Semaglutide and Tirzepatide have revolutionized weight loss treatment. These medications work by mimicking natural hormones that regulate appetite and blood sugar, helping you feel fuller longer and reducing cravings.

Key Benefits:

  • Improved metabolic health and blood sugar control
  • Reduced cardiovascular risk factors
  • Enhanced energy levels and quality of life

Lifestyle Integration:

Semaglutide vs Tirzepatide

Comparing Two Leading Weight Loss Medications

Both Semaglutide and Tirzepatide are highly effective GLP-1 receptor agonists, but they have unique characteristics that may make one more suitable for your needs.

Semaglutide

Brands: Ozempic, Wegovy

Mechanism: GLP-1 receptor agonist

Average Weight Loss: 15% of body weight

Dosing: Once weekly injection

Starting Price: From $299/month

Tirzepatide

Brand: Mounjaro, Zepbound

Mechanism: Dual GLP-1/GIP agonist

Average Weight Loss: 20% of body weight

Dosing: Once weekly injection

Starting Price: From $399/month

Key Differences:

Tirzepatide's dual-action mechanism targeting both GLP-1 and GIP receptors often results in slightly greater weight loss compared to Semaglutide. However, individual results vary, and factors like tolerance, side effects, and cost may influence which medication is best for you.

Understanding GLP-1 Medications

The Science Behind GLP-1 Receptor Agonists

GLP-1 (Glucagon-Like Peptide-1) medications represent a breakthrough in weight management and metabolic health, offering a scientifically-proven approach to sustainable weight loss.

How GLP-1 Medications Work

These medications mimic a natural hormone your body produces after eating. GLP-1 helps regulate:

  • Appetite: Increases feelings of fullness and reduces hunger
  • Blood Sugar: Stimulates insulin release when blood sugar is elevated
  • Digestion: Slows gastric emptying, helping you feel satisfied longer
  • Cravings: Reduces food-seeking behaviors and emotional eating

Available GLP-1 Medications

Semaglutide (Ozempic, Wegovy): FDA-approved for weight management, showing 15% average body weight reduction

Tirzepatide (Mounjaro, Zepbound): Dual GLP-1/GIP agonist with up to 20% weight loss results

Compounded GLP-1: Custom-formulated alternatives offering similar benefits at more affordable prices

Who Benefits Most?

GLP-1 medications are particularly effective for individuals with:

  • BMI of 30 or higher (obesity)
  • BMI of 27+ with weight-related health conditions
  • Difficulty losing weight through diet and exercise alone
  • Type 2 diabetes or prediabetes

What to Expect

Most patients start noticing reduced appetite within the first week. Weight loss typically becomes noticeable within 4-6 weeks, with maximum effects seen after several months of treatment. Side effects are usually mild and temporary, most commonly including nausea, which typically improves as your body adjusts.
