The GLP-1 Revolution

GLP-1 (glucagon-like peptide-1) medications were originally developed for type 2 diabetes. Researchers noticed patients were losing significant weight as a side effect. This led to dedicated weight loss formulations that have helped millions of people achieve results that diet and exercise alone couldn't deliver.

Available Weight Loss Medications

1. Semaglutide (Wegovy/Ozempic)

How it works: Weekly injection that activates GLP-1 receptors, reducing appetite and slowing stomach emptying.

Results: Average 15-17% body weight loss over 68 weeks

FDA Approved: 2021 for weight loss (Wegovy)

Cost: $1,300/month retail, from $299/month through telehealth

2. Tirzepatide (Zepbound/Mounjaro)

How it works: Dual-action medication activating both GLP-1 and GIP receptors

Results: Average 20-22% body weight loss over 72 weeks

FDA Approved: 2023 for weight loss (Zepbound)

Cost: $1,000/month retail, from $399/month through telehealth

3. Compounded GLP-1 Formulations

How it works: Same active ingredients as brand-name, made by licensed compounding pharmacies

Results: Similar to brand-name versions

Legal Status: Legal and widely used, not FDA-approved

Cost: From $199/month (50-70% savings)

Who is a Good Candidate?

You may qualify for GLP-1 weight loss medication if you have:

  • BMI of 30 or higher (obese)
  • BMI of 27-29.9 with weight-related health condition (diabetes, high blood pressure, sleep apnea)
  • History of unsuccessful weight loss with diet and exercise alone
  • Commitment to lifestyle changes alongside medication
  • No contraindications (severe GI disease, thyroid cancer history)

Side Effects and How to Manage Them

Most common side effects:

  • Nausea (20-40% of patients, usually mild)
  • Diarrhea (15-30%)
  • Constipation (10-25%)
  • Fatigue (10-20%)
  • Headache (10-15%)

Management strategies that work:

  • Start with lowest dose, increase slowly
  • Eat smaller, more frequent meals
  • Avoid fatty, greasy foods initially
  • Stay well-hydrated
  • Take anti-nausea medication if needed (your doctor can prescribe)
Reality Check: Most side effects are worst in weeks 1-4 and improve significantly as your body adjusts. By month 3, most patients have minimal to no side effects.
