Ozempic vs Wegovy - What's the Difference?

Same drug (Semaglutide), different indications:

Ozempic:

  • FDA-approved for type 2 diabetes
  • Maximum dose: 2mg weekly
  • Insurance usually covers for diabetes
  • Sometimes prescribed off-label for weight loss

Wegovy:

  • FDA-approved specifically for weight loss
  • Maximum dose: 2.4mg weekly (higher dose)

Which Should You Get? If you have type 2 diabetes, Ozempic (insurance may cover). If you're seeking weight loss only, Wegovy is the FDA-approved choice. Both work for weight loss - Wegovy just has the official indication.

Common Questions About Online Ozempic Prescriptions

Is it legal to get Ozempic online?

Yes, as long as you're consulting with a licensed physician in your state. Telemedicine is regulated and legal in all 50 states. MississippiDoc uses only Mississippi-licensed physicians for Mississippi residents.

Will I definitely get approved?

No. Approximately 15-20% of applicants are denied because they don't meet BMI requirements, have contraindications, or need different treatment approaches. A physician who approves everyone without proper review is practicing illegally.

How do I know the Ozempic is real?

Legitimate platforms use licensed US pharmacies that source directly from Novo Nordisk. If ordering compounded Semaglutide, verify the compounding pharmacy is licensed and inspected. Ask for pharmacy credentials.

What if I have side effects?

Your online physician is available for ongoing support. Most platforms offer messaging for questions about side effects, dose adjustments, or concerns. Severe side effects require immediate medical attention (call 911 or go to ER).

Can I use my insurance?

It depends. If the telehealth platform is in your insurance network and your plan covers Ozempic/Wegovy, you may be able to use insurance. However, many patients find cash-pay telehealth pricing is competitive with or cheaper than insurance copays.
