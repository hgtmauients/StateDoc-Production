Complete Guide to Non-GLP-1 Weight Loss Medications in New York

While GLP-1 medications like semaglutide and tirzepatide have dominated weight loss headlines, they're not the only effective options—and they're not right for everyone. If you're a New York resident exploring weight loss medications, several proven alternatives offer significant results at more accessible price points.

Why Consider Non-GLP-1 Alternatives?

There are several compelling reasons to explore options beyond GLP-1 medications:

  • Cost: Non-GLP-1 options range from $10-150/month versus $297-1,350/month for GLP-1s
  • Side effects: Some patients don't tolerate GLP-1 nausea and GI issues well
  • Medical contraindications: Personal or family history of thyroid cancer, pancreatitis, or other conditions that preclude GLP-1 use
  • Insurance coverage: Many plans cover non-GLP-1 options more readily
  • Different mechanisms: Alternative pathways may work better for specific metabolic profiles
  • Combination therapy: Can be paired with GLP-1s for enhanced results

Provider judgment determines which medication(s) are most appropriate for your situation.

Metformin: The Foundation Medication

How Metformin Works

Metformin is a diabetes medication that has proven weight loss benefits, particularly for patients with insulin resistance. It works by:

  • Reducing hepatic glucose production (liver makes less sugar)
  • Improving insulin sensitivity in muscle and fat tissue
  • Modestly suppressing appetite
  • Altering gut microbiome composition favorably
  • Reducing inflammation associated with metabolic syndrome

Expected Results

Average weight loss: 2-5% of body weight over 6-12 months

While modest compared to GLP-1s, metformin offers several advantages:

  • Excellent long-term safety profile (60+ years of use)
  • Very low cost ($10-30/month)
  • Well-tolerated by most patients
  • Improves metabolic markers (blood sugar, insulin, cholesterol)
  • May reduce cancer risk and extend healthspan
  • Can be combined with other weight loss medications

Who Benefits Most from Metformin?

Metformin is particularly effective for:

  • Patients with prediabetes or type 2 diabetes
  • Polycystic ovary syndrome (PCOS)
  • Insulin resistance (high fasting insulin, HOMA-IR)
  • Metabolic syndrome
  • Those seeking metabolic health improvements beyond weight loss

Dosing and Side Effects

Typical dosing: Start 500mg once daily with dinner, increase to 1,000-2,000mg daily in divided doses

Common side effects: GI disturbances (diarrhea, nausea) in 10-25% of patients, usually temporary. Extended-release formulation reduces side effects significantly.

Naltrexone-Bupropion (Contrave): Targeting Appetite and Reward

The Combination Approach

Contrave combines two medications that work synergistically on brain pathways:

Naltrexone: Opioid receptor antagonist that reduces food reward and cravings

Bupropion: Antidepressant that increases dopamine and norepinephrine, reducing appetite and improving mood

Expected Results

Average weight loss: 5-10% of body weight at one year

Clinical trials show:

  • 56% of patients lose =5% of body weight
  • 25% of patients lose =10% of body weight
  • Most effective when combined with lifestyle modifications
  • Results plateau around 6 months, then maintain

Who Benefits Most from Contrave?

Contrave is particularly effective for patients with:

  • Emotional eating or stress eating patterns
  • Food addiction or binge eating behaviors
  • Depression or low mood (dual benefit)
  • Strong food cravings and reward-driven eating
  • Former smokers (bupropion also helps with nicotine cravings)

Contraindications and Cautions

Do not use Contrave if you have:

  • Uncontrolled hypertension
  • Seizure disorders (bupropion lowers seizure threshold)
  • Eating disorders (anorexia, bulimia)
  • Opioid dependence or current opioid use
  • Abrupt discontinuation of alcohol or sedatives
  • MAOI use within 14 days

Dosing and Side Effects

Typical dosing: Gradual titration over 4 weeks to full dose of 32mg naltrexone + 360mg bupropion daily

Common side effects: Nausea (30-40%), headache (20%), constipation (15%), dizziness (10%), dry mouth (10%). Most side effects improve after first month.

Topiramate: The Appetite Suppressor

How Topiramate Works

Topiramate is an anticonvulsant used off-label for weight loss. Multiple mechanisms contribute to weight loss:

  • Potent appetite suppression
  • Altered taste perception (especially for sweets)
  • Increased calorie burning
  • Reduced food cravings

Expected Results

Average weight loss: 6-10% of body weight at one year

Some patients experience very significant weight loss (15-20%), particularly at higher doses. Response is individual and requires physician monitoring.

Who Benefits Most from Topiramate?

Topiramate is particularly effective for:

  • Patients with strong appetites resistant to other medications
  • Those with migraine headaches (dual benefit)
  • Binge eating disorder
  • Patients seeking significant weight loss at low cost

Important Considerations

Topiramate requires more careful monitoring than other options:

Slow titration is critical: Start very low (25mg) and increase gradually over 6-8 weeks to minimize side effects

Common side effects:

  • Cognitive effects ("brain fog," word-finding difficulty) in some patients
  • Paresthesias (tingling in hands/feet)
  • Taste changes (especially carbonation)
  • Kidney stone risk (increased fluid intake essential)

Contraindications: Pregnancy (increased birth defect risk), kidney disease, glaucoma

Phentermine: The Short-Term Catalyst

Powerful but Time-Limited

Phentermine is the most prescribed weight loss medication in the US due to its effectiveness and low cost. However, it's only FDA-approved for short-term use (3 months at a time).

How Phentermine Works

Phentermine is a sympathomimetic amine that:

  • Stimulates norepinephrine release
  • Strongly suppresses appetite
  • Increases energy expenditure
  • Provides mild stimulant effect

Expected Results

Average weight loss: 5-10% over 3 months

Phentermine is most effective as a catalyst to:

  • Jump-start weight loss before transitioning to long-term therapy
  • Break through weight loss plateaus
  • Provide intense appetite control during initial diet adjustments
  • Build momentum and confidence for ongoing weight management

Who Benefits Most from Phentermine?

Phentermine works well for:

  • Patients needing rapid initial results for motivation
  • Those with very strong appetites
  • Patients preparing for surgery or other time-sensitive goals
  • Intermittent use for maintaining long-term weight loss

Cautions and Contraindications

Do not use phentermine if you have:

  • Cardiovascular disease or uncontrolled hypertension
  • Hyperthyroidism
  • Glaucoma
  • History of substance abuse
  • Anxiety disorders (may worsen)
  • MAOI use within 14 days

Common side effects: Increased heart rate, elevated blood pressure, insomnia, dry mouth, restlessness, anxiety

Comparing Your Options

Quick Comparison Table

Contrave: 5-10% weight loss, $75-150/month, FDA-approved for chronic use, best for emotional eating

Topiramate: 6-10% weight loss, $15-50/month, requires monitoring, best for strong appetites

Combination Approaches

Many physicians use combination therapy for enhanced results:

Phentermine + Topiramate (Qsymia)

FDA-approved combination with 10-15% average weight loss. More effective than either drug alone. Available as brand (expensive) or individual generics (affordable).

Metformin + GLP-1

Metformin enhances GLP-1 effectiveness and addresses insulin resistance. Common combination for diabetic patients.

Metformin + Contrave

Addresses both metabolic dysfunction and appetite/reward pathways. Well-tolerated combination.

Provider judgment determines appropriate combination therapy based on individual patient factors.

Maximizing Results

Lifestyle Integration

Weight loss medications are most effective when combined with sustainable lifestyle changes:

Nutrition Foundation

  • Protein-focused eating (25-30% of calories)
  • Whole, minimally processed foods
  • Adequate fiber (25-35g daily)
  • Consistent meal timing
  • Mindful eating practices

Movement and Exercise

  • Resistance training 2-3x/week (preserve muscle during weight loss)
  • Daily walking or other moderate activity
  • Gradual intensity increases as fitness improves
  • Find activities you enjoy for long-term adherence

Behavioral Strategies

  • Food logging or tracking
  • Regular weigh-ins and measurements
  • Stress management techniques
  • Adequate sleep (7-9 hours)
  • Social support systems
