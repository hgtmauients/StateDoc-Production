Hair Loss Treatments That Actually Work (2025)

Only 3 treatments have FDA approval and clinical evidence for hair regrowth: Finasteride, Minoxidil, and low-level laser therapy. Everything else is either experimental, ineffective, or a scam. Here's the complete evidence-based guide to what actually works for male pattern baldness.

The Only 3 FDA-Approved Treatments

1. Finasteride (Propecia) - Most Effective

How it works: Blocks DHT (hormone that causes hair loss in genetically susceptible men)

Effectiveness:

  • Stops hair loss in 90% of men
  • Regrows hair in 65% of men
  • Results visible in 6-12 months
  • Requires ongoing use to maintain

Dosing: 1mg daily oral tablet

Cost: $20-30/month (generic), $500/month (brand Propecia)

Side effects:

  • Sexual side effects in 2-4% of users (reversible)
  • Most men tolerate it perfectly
  • Effects resolve if medication stopped

2. Minoxidil (Rogaine) - Most Accessible

How it works: Increases blood flow to hair follicles, extends growth phase

Effectiveness:

  • Stops hair loss in 60-70% of men
  • Regrows hair in 40-50%
  • Results in 4-8 months
  • Works best on crown, less on hairline

Dosing: Apply 5% solution/foam twice daily to scalp

Cost: $10-30/month

Side effects:

  • Scalp irritation (uncommon)
  • Initial shedding (first 2-4 weeks, then regrowth)
  • Facial hair growth if drips on face

3. Low-Level Laser Therapy - Newest Approval

How it works: Red light stimulates cellular activity in follicles

Effectiveness:

  • Increases hair density by 15-25%
  • Works in 40-60% of users
  • Results in 6-12 months
  • Must continue treatment to maintain

Cost: $200-500 for device (one-time), $2,000-4,000 for professional treatments

Evidence-Based Truth: Finasteride + Minoxidil combination is the most effective approach. Studies show 85-90% of men maintain or improve hair using both together.

What Doesn't Work (Save Your Money)

No clinical evidence:

  • Biotin supplements (doesn't prevent genetic hair loss)
  • Most shampoos (marketing hype)
  • Scalp massages (feels good, doesn't regrow hair)
  • Essential oils (no scientific support)
  • Most OTC supplements (waste of money)
Warning: Hair loss industry is filled with scams. If a product promises "miracle regrowth" without FDA approval, it's likely fraudulent. Stick to evidence-based treatments.

Timeline: When Will You See Results?

Finasteride Timeline:

  • Month 1-3: Hair loss may initially increase (shedding)
  • Month 4-6: Shedding stops, stabilization
  • Month 6-12: New growth becomes visible
  • Month 12-24: Maximum results achieved

Minoxidil Timeline:

  • Month 1-2: Initial shedding (normal, temporary)
  • Month 3-4: Shedding stops
  • Month 4-8: New fine hairs appear
  • Month 8-12: Hair becomes thicker and more noticeable
